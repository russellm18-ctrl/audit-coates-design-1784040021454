// Findings

## Atmospheric Reality Gap

Your four hero images transmit a coherent emotional register — cinematic, deliberate, heritage-weighted — and they do it without a single wasted frame. The Münster street shot opens with anonymity and scale: a figure dwarfed by centuries of stone, facing forward, never looking back at you. That is a brand posture, not a photograph. The archival book frame reinforces it: a hand turning pages of black-and-white architectural photography signals that your reference points predate trends by decades. The drill-press craftsman, lit with warm practical light against a dark shop floor, closes the loop between historical authority and present-day making. These three images earn "Beyond All Measure | Since 1681."

The logo card does not. Dropped between atmospheric cinematography, a parchment-textured brand lockup reads as a placeholder slide in a pitch deck — it interrupts the sensory sequence rather than deepening it. The other frames pull the buyer *into* a world; the logo card stops them at the door and hands them a business card instead. In a brand that stakes everything on continuity and craft, a gap in atmospheric consistency is perceived as a gap in conviction. That perception costs you the buyer who was almost sold.

## Visual Narrative Coherence

You rank #1 organically for "Metrica" — every click that lands on your homepage hits the full weight of those four frames. The problem is what happens the moment a buyer moves deeper. Your image filenames — `Heritage-02.jpg`, `Metrica-Hero.jpg`, `Enduring-Legacy-Hero.jpg`, `42.jpg`, `17.jpg`, `Workshop-04.jpg` — reveal a segmented production workflow, not a unified visual system. Files named `42.jpg` and `17.jpg` signal asset management, not authorship. A procurement director comparing you against a competitor whose every image filename reads like a chapter title registers this dissonance subconsciously: the story felt intentional on the surface, then got sloppy underneath.

Zero broken links across 25 checked is operationally clean — nothing is actively hemorrhaging traffic. But the scrape also surfaced a Vimeo-hosted video infrastructure (`/playback/1191212482`, `/1191212485`, `/1191212484`) serving three section hero videos. If Vimeo's CDN hiccups or a contract lapses, your entire atmospheric opening disappears simultaneously with no fallback. A brand built on 340 years of continuity cannot afford a single-vendor dependency on its first impression.

## Metadata and Conversion Gaps

This is where the #1 ranking becomes expensive to waste. Your page title is "Metrica | Home" — 14 characters that tell a search engine and every social preview exactly nothing about what you make, how long you have made it, or why that matters. Your meta description is 4 characters. Four. Whatever copy lives there is invisible to every platform that reads it. And your `og:image` is missing entirely, which means every LinkedIn share, every Slack paste, every text message with your URL generates a blank preview card — the visual brand that took cinematographers and editors to build disappears the moment someone tries to share it.

Of your 20 homepage content images, 1 is missing alt text — a 5% gap that is minor in volume but symbolically inconsistent for a brand claiming precision as its identity. More critically, the missing `og:image` means the craftsman shot, the Münster frame, the archival hand — none of them travel with your URL. You are paying for photography that stops working the moment it leaves your server.

Fix the metadata before you add a single new image. The photography is already doing its job; the infrastructure is refusing to let it travel.
